# Actually learning stuff
## Repeaters
I got bored of nonexistant simplex, so I decided maybe I should try some of the local repeaters I keep hearing. [RepeaterBook](https://www.repeaterbook.com/) has been probably one of ***THE MOST*** useful resources I have found throughout my radio journey. This is essentially a database of repeaters near you and their downlink, offsets, and all sorts of other info you'd normally need to spy out to find.

After a bit I got inputing repeaters on my icom ID-52 down to a process. I discovered later on that this is very similar for almost any radio, so here is a basic rundown:

### Programming a repeater without weird software (I know nothing yet about the apps)
What you will need the following, which can be obtained from [RepeaterBook](https://www.repeaterbook.com/):
> Note: this is for a subaudible tone, DCS is something I know very little about, but it should probably follow a similar process

- Downlink
- ± Frequency Offset (yesssss I got to use the symbol)
- Uplink Tone (or just "Tone")

You should also make sure that the repeater is open for public access.

Once you have these details, do the following:
Go to the downlink frequency. Then, in at least one of your radio's menus or settings panes the following can be configured:
- Tone, or uplink tone: the frequency in Hz that the website gave you
    - also make sure you turn on this tone (this was through the `QUICK` menu)
- Frequency offset: the other frequncy (probably in KHz), ***make sure you do the correct + or - value***, sometimes this is configured in a different menu (in my case it was the `QUICK` button)
    - again, make sure you turn duplex on, this was through the `QUICK` menu as well on my icom

#### Example
Website data:
Downlink: 146.64000
Offset: -0.600 (doesn't say it, but this is KHz)
Uplink Tone: 123.0 (again, doesn't say it, but this is Hz)

This one appears to be Both On-Air and Open, and it allows **FM** (our desired transmission type).

## Using the repeater
People seem to be intimidated (I was too) by transmitting on a repeater, there is a lot going on. Here is are some basic rules:

### Basic repeater etiquette
> Always follow FCC rules (or your local country's if you are outside of the US)

- Don't purpously transmit over somebody (duh).
    - (sane) people won't get mad at you if it's an accident
- Don't call "CQ" on a repeater, just say "\[callsign\], monitoring"
    - also don't say your monitoring multiple times, it can be annoying
    - wait until you hear someone else online and start some awkward small talk (yayyyyyyyyyy)

That's really about it. If you want to be propper, when you want to log off just say "\[callsign\], clear" (clear, means you are logging off)
